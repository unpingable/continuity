## The seam

Robust aggregation differentiates participants; privacy decides who learns the
discriminant; **neither answers who authored the coordinate system.** A proof
*satisfies* a predicate; it does not *authorize* it (`signed ≠ witnessed`,
applied to categories). A predicate is a declared state requiring a witness, and
that witness has provenance, scope, and **freshness**.

## Continuity's role

Continuity preserves *what can be relied on* — and in this chain that means
preserving, with replay boundaries: **which predicate, which witness, which
admission, which use, which version.** Its refusal:

> **A predicate-witness is not immortal.** "We admitted this predicate once" must
> not become standing folklore. A stale or superseded predicate-witness is not
> silently reused; freshness and version are custody, not metadata.

Without this, a privacy-preserving classifier's admission becomes permanent the
moment it is sealed — the most disguised failure mode, because the cryptography
makes the (possibly stale, possibly unwitnessed) category feel objective.
Continuity is where `proof-satisfaction ≠ durable-authority` is kept honest over
time.
